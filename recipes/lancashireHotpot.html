Lancashire Hotpot Recipe

Description

Lancashire Hotpot is a traditional British dish made with lamb, onions, and sliced potatoes, all slow-cooked in a hearty broth. It’s a warming, wholesome meal that’s perfect for a chilly evening.

Ingredients

  • 1 kg lamb shoulder, cut into chunks
  • 2 onions, sliced
  • 4 large potatoes, sliced thinly
  • 500ml lamb or beef stock
  • 2 sprigs of fresh thyme
  • 2 tablespoons butter
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Steps

  1. Preheat the oven to 170°C (340°F).
  2. In a large ovenproof dish, layer the lamb and onions.
  3. Season with salt, pepper, and thyme.
  4. Pour the stock over the lamb and onions.
  5. Arrange the sliced potatoes on top, overlapping slightly.
  6. Brush the potato slices with melted butter and season with salt.
  7. Cover the dish with foil and bake for 1 hour.
  8. Remove the foil and bake for another 30 minutes or until the potatoes are golden and crispy.
  9. Let it rest for a few minutes before serving.
